**Source**: QuantumScape, Toyota, Samsung SDI.
**Core Tech**: Lithium-Metal Anode with Solid Ceramic Separator (No liquid electrolyte).

## The Discovery

Replacing the flammable liquid electrolyte with a solid ceramic allows for lithium-metal anodes. This doubles energy density (to ~1000 Wh/L) and enables charging 0-80% in 15 minutes without fire risk.

## The State of Art (2026)

- **QuantumScape QSE-5**: Shipping commercial validation cells (A-sample/B-sample). 800+ Wh/L.
- **Toyota**: "Performance" Li-Ion in 2026, Solid-State in 2027 (target 900 mile range).
- **Manufacturing**: The "Cobra" separator process is the key bottleneck breaker, allowing roll-to-roll manufacturing of ceramics.

## Key Players

1. **QuantumScape**: The tech leader (backed by VW).
2. **Toyota**: The patent volume leader.
3. **Solid Power**: The "sulfide-based" alternative (BMW/Ford).
